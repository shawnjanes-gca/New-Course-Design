Case Study 3 - Income Tax Advantages Over Time

Over time, the advantage of the foundation grows because private foundations are not subject to income tax. Properly managed, private foundations pay only a minimal excise tax of 2% on investment income. Let us illustrate the effect of these tax savings by expanding the previous example.

Case Study 3 - Income Tax Advantages Over Time

Suppose Mr. Johansenn is 55 years old and has assets of $12,500,000, which return 8% a year net pre-tax, giving him $1,000,000 of income. Also assume that each year he contributes 30% of his income to his foundation, his foundation earns 8%, makes the required minimum annual distribution, and pays excise taxes of 2% of investment income and foundation management fees of 1% of assets. Assuming all income is interest income and ignoring any living expenses and computing his taxes at a flat 45%, after 27 years his net worth will be $28.4 million, and his foundation will be worth $16.3 million, for a total of $44.7 million.

Contrast that situation to one in which the client has no foundation but makes the same charitable contributions as his foundation would have made. He will have $33.3 million after 27 years. Thus, during the client's life, he can increase the assets under his control by over $11.4 million just by funding his foundation annually.
