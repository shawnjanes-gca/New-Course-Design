Limits on Tax Deductibility of Donations

There are limitations on the amount of the charitable deduction that can be taken in a given year from income taxes for donations to a private foundation. Donations that exceed these limits may be carried forward for income tax deductions in future years.

These limits are as follows. Click each limit to learn more.

≡

1. Limited to 30% of AGI

Deductions for contributions to private foundations are limited to 30 percent of the donor's annual adjusted gross income (AGI). This 30% limit is an overall limit.

2. Long-term appreciated publicly traded stock limited to 20% of AGI

Donors contributing long-term appreciated publicly traded stock to a private foundation may deduct such contributions up to 20% of their adjusted gross income (AGI). To receive a deduction based on fair market value, the donor must have held the stock for more than one year, and the stock must be traded on an exchange.

In other words, the deduction on combined contributions of all types is capped at 30% of AGI, of which no more than 20% of AGI can be for contributions of long-term appreciated property. The deductibility of contributions beyond these limits can be carried over for 5 years.

For example, a donor may get a deduction for up to 30% of AGI by giving 30% in cash. If the donor gives long-term appreciated property instead of cash, then only 20% of AGI could be deducted. If, however, the donor gives10% in cash and 20% in long-term appreciated property, then the combined contributions would be deductible because they do not exceed 30% of AGI [ref. Sec. 170(b)(1)(D)].
