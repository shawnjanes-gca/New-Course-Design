Tax Returns
Even though charities generally pay no income tax, they are still required to file income tax returns with the IRS. Private foundations must file a Form 990-PF annually, in addition to certain other forms that may be required from time to time. Portions of the 990-PF are public and must be made available to the public on demand.

The requirement to file tax returns means that very precise accounting is required for private foundations. Even though the amount of tax (from the 2% or 1% excise tax on net investment income) is almost always small, the rules still require that it be calculated properly and paid on a timely basis.

  

Net investment income

Net investment income is defined as total investment income less expenses required to collect or produce the income. Investment management fees represent an expense required to produce income.
