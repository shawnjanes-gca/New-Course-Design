Financial Benefits of Private Foundations

While private foundations offer a number of benefits, tax benefits alone are not a sufficient reason to create a foundation. A would-be foundation creator must be interested in supporting charity, either immediately or at some time. But given some charitable intent on the part of the founder, there are a number of substantial financial benefits to creating a foundation.

Among the financial benefits of private foundations are the following:

1. Immediate income tax deduction

2. Avoid capital gains tax

3. Income-tax-free growth

4. Complete avoidance of all transfer taxes
 
Income-tax-free growth - Private foundations may earn income without owing income tax, providing the ability to grow assets in the foundation on a tax-free basis. Tax-free compounding, of course, is a very powerful tool that facilitates long-term growth of assets, thereby allowing the grantor to make more significant grants in the future.
Avoid capital gains tax - If the contribution is made in the form of long-term appreciated stock, the donor does not have to pay capital gains taxes on the contributions, thereby maximizing the value of the gift.
Immediate income tax deduction - One of the most important and often recognized benefits of a private foundation is the immediate income tax deduction that can be realized from the assets transferred to these vehicles. However, there are certain limits associated with this benefit that are discussed on the next page
Complete avoidance of all transfer taxes - All contributions to qualified charities are exempt from transfer taxes. This includes contributions to private foundations. Hence, all contributions, whether made during life or upon death, to private foundations are outside of the donor's estate, and therefore free of any gift tax, estate tax, or generation skipping tax. This is particularly important when considering the long-term intergenerational accumulation of wealth for charitable use.
