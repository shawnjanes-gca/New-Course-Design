Situations in Which Creating a Foundation Might Be Especially Appropriate

The following is a list of specific economic situations that particularly suggest creating and funding a private foundation. Clients who experience any of these economic situations should consider a private foundation:

Appropriate Situations

  1. A particularly high income in a year;
  2. Steady, or growing, income above personal needs;
  3. An intent to endow a private foundation at death;
  4. Client currently gives a significant amount to charity each year;
  5. Client owns highly appreciated, publicly traded stock;
  6. Client's net worth exceeds the amount he or she anticipates needing;
  7. Ownership of the appreciated stock of a particularly volatile company;
  8. Ownership of a large block of appreciated, thinly traded public stock;
  9. A plan to leave a substantial amount of assets to charity upon death.
