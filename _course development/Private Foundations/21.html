Turn-Key Professional Management Makes Smaller Foundations Feasible

As this course has demonstrated, the administrative and compliance burden of a private foundation can be considerable. However, if a professional firm is retained to provide turnkey management, the process is actually very simple for both advisor and client. To create a foundation, the client really only needs to make three simple decisions. These are:

Founder's Key Decisions

  1. Decide who is to serve on the board. Only on person is needed, and there is no upper limit. Typically, it is recommended that a founder's initial board consist of the founder, and optionally a spouse and/or children.
  2. Decide the initial funding. Creating a foundation in one year does not obligate the founder to contribute to it in future years.
  3. Decide what to call the foundation. Almost any name will work, provided it is available. Quite often, the founder's name or family name is used.
