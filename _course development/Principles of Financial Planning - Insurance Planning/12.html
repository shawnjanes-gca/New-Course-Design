Permanent Life Insurance

In contrast to term life insurance, permanent life insurance offers two key benefits:

  • Lifetime coverage - Not just for a specified term.
  • Cash Surrender Value (CSV) - Supplements life insurance coverage with a savings element. As premiums are paid, an accumulated savings amount (known as the policy's cash value) gradually builds.

Through the years, as the investment and savings aspect of life insurance became more important in the market, permanent life insurance evolved into the three basic types listed below. Click each term to learn more.

1. Traditional Whole Life

Traditional whole life insurance provides lifetime insurance coverage with premiums that do not increase over time (a level premium rate). The policy also builds cash value (surrender value), which is distributed to the insured if the policy is terminated prior to death.

2. Universal Life

This type of insurance unbundles the pricing factors of the policy; therefore, it may provide the purchaser with more flexibility regarding payment of premiums and face amounts.

Universal Life can be maintained without cash value as long as the premiums are sufficient to cover the mortality and administration expense.Â  If the policy has cash value sufficient to pay the mortality charges and expenses, then premiums can generally be paid at the will of the owner.

3. Variable Life

This type of insurance allows the purchaser to assume full investment risk of the policy. The owner can allocate premiums among a number of funds, and the death benefit and cash value will vary with the performance of those funds.
