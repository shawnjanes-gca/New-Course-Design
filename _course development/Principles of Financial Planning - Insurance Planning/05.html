The Wealth Accumulation Stage

At each stage of the financial lifecycle, individuals are exposed to various risk factors.

Click each need to learn more.

 

Low Cost, Instantaneous Estate

If death occurs in the wealth accumulation stage, there may be insufficient funds to replace lost income or to cover increased expenses associated with the surviving spouse needing assistance with childcare. Thus, there is a need for immediate liquidity that can enhance the size of the estate upon death to provide the necessary funds.

Tax-Deferred Savings

Most people are aware of the advantages of tax-deferred savings in the form of IRAs, 401(k), or 403(b) plans. But such plans are often inadequate, and individuals are in need of alternatives that provide tax-deferred savings.

Income Replacement

One of the greatest risks during the wealth accumulation phase is the loss of the ability to earn money. There needs to be a way to replace income if someone becomes disabled.

Retirement Planning

At this point in the lifecycle, the future is filled with many contingencies. Planning early to minimize the risks of the unexpected can help secure a future with a comfortable retirement.

Liability Coverage

Liability Coverage includes college funding, mortgage balances, and large debts.

It is typical in this stage to have liabilities that are greater than total assets. An unexpected death or disability can cause those obligations to become overwhelming, and literally force a family from their home.
