The Wealth Maintenance Stage

To get a better understanding of the needs and objectives of people in the Wealth Maintenance Stage, review each need below.

Click each need to learn more.

 

Expense Planning

Expense planning includes estate taxes, college funding, and long-term care.

One of the primary threats to wealth maintenance is sizeable expenses that go beyond the day-to-day expenses, particularly those that are unexpected. Long-term care for a family member can threaten the financial security that has been established, as can college tuition. And at this stage, the estate is large enough that planning to minimize potential estate taxes becomes a priority.

Earnings Replacement

While current earnings may or may not be adding to wealth, they are typically maintaining the day-to-day costs of living so that the accumulated wealth can be preserved. But any loss of earnings, even short-term, could significantly affect savings and jeopardize one's current standard of living.
