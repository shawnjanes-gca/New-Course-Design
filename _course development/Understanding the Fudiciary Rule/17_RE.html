Review Exercise

1.

You are a commission-based Registered Representative. You recommended and sold an investment to your client Marie for her taxable account after April 10, 2017. Please indicate true or false for each of the following statements:

You owe Marie a fiduciary standard of care under The Rule.

Incorrect. Marie is not a Retail Retirement Investor because taxable accounts are not covered by The Rule. Consequently, you are not a Fiduciary Advisor. However, you are a Registered Representative and you owe Marie a Suitability Standard of care under FINRA.
Correct. Marie is not a Retail Retirement Investor because taxable accounts are not covered by The Rule; Consequently, you are not a Fiduciary Advisor. However, you are a Registered Representative; You owe Marie a Suitability Standard of care under FINRA.

You are allowed to have conflicts of interest with Marie in connection with her taxable account.

Correct. The Suitability Standard does not prohibit conflicts of interest.
Incorrect. The Suitability Standard does not prohibit conflicts of interest.

You may receive variable compensation from sales of financial products or services into Marie’s taxable account.

Correct. You are not a Fiduciary Advisor and are therefore not subject to The Rule. Instead, you are subject to FINRA rules that generally permit variable compensation within FINRA’s fairness and reasonability doctrines.
Incorrect. You are not a Fiduciary Advisor and are therefore not subject to The Rule. Instead, you are subject to FINRA rules that generally permit variable compensation within FINRA’s fairness and reasonability doctrines.

2.

Mr. Seguro D. Vida, life insurance professional, recommended a 401(k) rollover to his client Patti after April 10, 2017. At Seguro’s recommendation, the funds were rolled over into an Individual Retirement Annuity to purchase a variable annuity for Patti’s benefit. Seguro will earn a 7% commission from the sale of the variable annuity. He does not earn compensation from the assets in Patti’s 401(k) Plan. Which of the following statements is false?

Incorrect. The statement is true. Rollover recommendations are considered investment advice.
Incorrect. The statement is true. Compensation that varies based either on the amount or type of product sold is variable compensation.
Incorrect. The statement is true. Owners of IRAs are Retail Retirement Investors.
Correct. The statement is false. Variable annuities and indexed annuities are not exempt from The Rule. Only fixed annuities are exempt.

3.

Which of the following is least likely to represent conflicted advice under The Rule? Assume (1) all recommendations are made after the applicability date of The Rule and (2) all of the individuals making recommendations are Fiduciary Advisors.

Incorrect. The higher the purchase amount, the higher the commission. The recommendation is conflicted advice.
Incorrect. The higher the purchase amount, the higher the Revenue Share. In addition, the revenue share is third-party compensation. Therefore the recommendation is conflicted advice.
Incorrect. Indexed and variable annuity recommendations are subject to The Rule (fixed annuities are not subject to The Rule). The higher the purchase amount, the higher the commission. The recommendation is conflicted advice.
Correct! The Investment Advisor Representative’s recommendations are unlikely to be influenced by compensation and are not considered conflicted advice. Noteworthy - owners of Health Savings Accounts are Retail Retirement Investors.
