Who is a Covered Advisor?

A covered advisor may be a person or a firm. However, not all advisors are “covered” advisors. You are generally a covered advisor if any one of the following applies to you:

≡

FIDUCIARY STATUS

You acknowledge fiduciary status to a client or represent yourself as a fiduciary to a client.1

AGREEMENT

There is an agreement or understanding (written or oral) in place under which you agree to deliver investment advice to your client based upon his or her particular investment needs.

Advice

You direct (provide) advice to a specific person regarding the advisability of a particular (specific) investment decision.

Let us assume that you are a covered advisor. You will become a Fiduciary Advisor only if you make a covered recommendation as explained on the next page.

1 The term “client” in this context means “advice recipient” and includes clients, prospects, and literally anyone to whom you deliver investment advice or acknowledge fiduciary status.
