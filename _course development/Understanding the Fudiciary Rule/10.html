BIC Lite– Level Fee Fiduciary Exemption (LFFE)

The Level Fee Fiduciary Exemption (LFFE) empowers you to earn a level fee from ERISA plan rollovers and IRAs without the complexity of a Full BICE. As but one insight, the LFFE does not require a signed Best Interests Contract whereas the Full BICE does!

The mere discussion of a rollover with a client may be conflicted advice. Hence, the LFFE generally covers a recommendation with respect to an ERISA plan or IRA rollover or distribution. When the assets rollover to a level fee arrangement, the potential for conflicted advice drops dramatically and, accordingly, the Full BICE is not required.

Exempting level fees is hardly a new ERISA concept. Registered Investment Advisors1 have charged level advisory fees since the enabling provisions of The Pension Protection Act of 2006. In our post-DOL apocalyptic world the LFFE requires the following fee structure:

  • An advisory fee that is fixed in (1) percentage or (2) amount.
  • The Fiduciary Advisor, financial institution, or third party affiliate receives no other direct or indirect compensation.
  • Neither proprietary products nor products generating third party compensation are allowed.
  • No trading commissions may be paid to the Fiduciary Advisor. The use of a trade away or wrap fee may be appropriate.

Why has the DOL opened wide the door for level fees? The fee paid to the Fiduciary Advisor or financial institution does not vary based upon investment recommendations; Therefore the advice is unlikely to be conflicted.

FIDUCIARY STATUS

The Fiduciary Advisor and financial institution must acknowledge, in writing, fiduciary status to the Retail Retirement Investor before the transaction. The acknowledgement may be made through written disclosure. A written contract is not required as with the Full BICE.

IMPARTIAL CONDUCT STANDARDS

Advisor and firm must comply with the “Impartial Conduct Standards” discussed previously. Notably, the level fee must be reasonable (not excessive) under these standards.

IRAs AND ROLLOVERS BEST INTEREST DOCUMENTATION

If the covered recommendation relates to IRA and qualified plan transactions, the financial institution must justify and document that the following recommendations are in the best interests of the Retail Retirement Investor:

  • Rollover or distribution from an ERISA plan to an IRA
  • IRA to IRA rollovers or distributions
  • Change a commission-based account to a level fee account

The BICE and LFFE are foundational to understanding The Rule. A quick review of key distinctions between the two may crystalize how these exemptions function.

After executing the LFFE, advice is deemed not conflicted and a Prohibited Transaction Exemption (PTE) is no longer required.
