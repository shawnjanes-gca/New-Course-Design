Review Exercise

1.

Ms. Ophelia Octogenarian is a vivacious 81-year-old. She called you on April 11, 2017 to request investment advice for $100,000 of her IRA balance. She has shared nothing of her financial profile, existing portfolio size/allocation, or risk tolerance. You will be compensated for any products she purchases based upon your recommendation. You are about to recommend an indexed annuity to Ophelia. Which of the following, if any, would make your recommendation prudent?

Incorrect. You do not know if she has a need for more income.
Incorrect. This is a generalization. She may be less conservative than her peers.
Incorrect. Her current liquidity has not been established.
Incorrect. Her health or other relevant factors have not been discussed.
Correct. You must know far more about Ophelia’s financial dynamic to make a prudent recommendation. We know nothing of the amount and type of her other assets, the income currently being generated by all investments, Social Security income, need for liquidity, or risk tolerance.

2.

You are a Fiduciary Advisor. Which of the following, if any, recommendations may be made under a BICE? All of the recommendations below are made after the applicability date.

Incorrect. A BIC may not be used where the Fiduciary Advisor has discretionary control over ERISA plan or IRA assets.
Incorrect. A BIC cannot be used for transactions between the fiduciary advisor personally and ERISA plan or IRA. This kind of transaction is strictly prohibited.
Incorrect. A BIC may not include language that places burdensome restrictions on the right to arbitration.
Incorrect. A BIC may not include language that places limitations on the rights of the IRA Retail Retirement Investor to participate in a class action lawsuit.
Correct.
