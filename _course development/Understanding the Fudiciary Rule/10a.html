LFFE vs. Full BICE

The BICE and LFFE are foundational to understanding The Rule. A quick review of key distinctions between the two may crystalize how these exemptions function.

LFFE vs. Full BICE
Distinction\Exemption LFFE Full BICE
Signed BIC Required? No Yes1
Acknowledge Fiduciary Status Yes Yes
Comply with Impartial Conduct Standards Yes Yes
Proprietary Products Not Allowed Allowed with additional disclosure
Major Additional Conditions Document how ERISA plan and IRA rollovers or distributions fit the Best Interest Standard
  • Written affirmative warranties
  • Written disclosure (initial and transactional)
  • Website disclosures
  • Notice to the DOL
  • Stringent recordkeeping
Note

After executing the LFFE, advice is deemed not conflicted and a Prohibited Transaction Exemption (PTE) is no longer required.

1 IRA owners require a signed BIC while ERISA participants require only acknowledgement of fiduciary status and certain disclosures.
