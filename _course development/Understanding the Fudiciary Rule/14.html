PRINCIPAL TRANSACTIONS EXEMPTION

Under The Rule, neither a Fiduciary Advisor nor a financial institution is generally allowed to act as a principal by selling financial products from its own inventory to Retail Retirement Investors. The DOL recognizes the potential for conflicted advice in principal transactions and therefore limits their use.

A Fiduciary Advisor or financial institution acting as a principal may sell only certain debt securities1, CD’s and unit investment trusts from its own inventory to Retail Retirement Investors under this exemption.

While this Prohibited Transaction Exemption is a separate exemption from the BICE, adherence to Impartial Conduct Standards, adoption of polices to reduce the risk of conflicted advice, and additional disclosures are required.

Transition BICE

The Transition BICE is a scaled down version of the Full BICE; It provides relief during the transition period from April 10, 2017 through January 1, 2018.  Specifically, the documentation of compliance policies and other disclosure requirements of the Full BICE are delayed until January 1, 2018. However, virtually all other BICE requirements remain firmly in place.   

Please Note

The Transition BICE is temporary and allows the Financial Institution more time to fully comply with the requirements of the BICE.

Insurance and Annuity Exemption

This exemption is separate from the BICE and is actually a modification of an exemption in effect before The Rule. Life insurance, variable annuities, indexed annuities, and fixed annuities could generally be sold to ERISA plans before the rule. Effective with the applicability date, only life insurance and fixed annuities may be sold to Retail Retirement Investors under this exemption. Indexed and variable annuity sales are not eligible, but may be eligible through the BICE.

Please Note

The sale of variable or indexed annuities will generally require a BICE.

Alternative Fee Structure (AFS)

This exemption was in effect before The Rule2 and remains a valid exemption. The AFS exemption reduces the potential for conflicted advice by (1) placing caps on the amount of variable compensation that can be earned3, (2) rebating fees that violate the reasonable compensation standard, and/or (3) allowing performance-based fees. Enhanced disclosure is required if recommendations are limited in whole or in part to proprietary products.

BICE Carve-Outs

The following transactions and communications are not considered investment advice and therefore the advisor is not a Fiduciary Advisor when performing them.

Sophisticated Counterparty

Recommendations made to the following sophisticated parties acting as independent fiduciaries will generally not be considered “investment advice” under The Rule:

  • Bank
  • Insurance Company
  • Registered Investment Advisor
  • Registered Broker-Dealer
  • Any independent fiduciary with at least $50MM under management

In-House Communications

An employee of the plan sponsor may communicate with plan participants. Such communications are generally not considered investment advice.

1U.S. corporate, U.S. government, and U.S. government agency bonds

2ERISA Section 408(b)(14)

3The Fiduciary Advisor and Financial Institution may not earn compensation in addition to alternative fees. However, this additional compensation may be directed to a separate affiliate.
