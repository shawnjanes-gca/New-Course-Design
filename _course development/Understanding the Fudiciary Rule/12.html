Grandfathered Exemption (GE)

The Grandfathered Exemption(GE), technically referred to as the Pre-Existing Transactions Exemption, was initially heralded as a potential “loophole” to The Rule. A closer examination of the GE dashed the hope that Fiduciary Advisors and financial institutions could continue offering conflicted advice without restriction. Specifically, the GE is constrained by the following:

<
>
  • Timing
  • Reasonable Compensation
  • Impartial Conduct
  • Grandfathered Transactions

Existing variable compensation may continue only if based upon an agreement (written or verbal) with the following characteristics:

  • The agreement was made before the applicability date.
  • The agreement is currently valid and has not expired.
  • If expired and subsequently renewed, the renewal occurred before the applicability date.

Total compensation paid to the Fiduciary Advisor must be reasonable, i.e. “not excessive.”

The term “compensation” in this context includes variable direct compensation from the client and indirect compensation from third party sources.

Variable compensation is simply compensation that varies based upon the type or amount of financial product sold.  Examples include up front commissions, trailing commissions, deferred sales charges, and 12(b)-1 fees.

A third party source is any source other than directly from the client.  Examples include revenue sharing and soft dollar arrangements.

The Impartial Conduct Standards apply to recommendations made AFTER the applicability date. 

The GE allows variable compensation from:

  • Sales of financial products or services pursuant to recommendations made before the applicability date
  • Systematic investing into specific products that began before the applicability date and continuing beyond that date
  • Rebalancing of a portfolio after the applicability date under an agreement existing before the applicability date1

Implementation of the GE is simple. A negative consent is sent to your client. If the client does nothing, he or she is deemed to have consented to the GE. Clients must affirmatively opt out of the GE if they do not wish to agree to its terms.

1Compensation generally may not increase based upon the rebalancing.
