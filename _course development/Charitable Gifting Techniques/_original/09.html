Two Types of Charitable Remainder Trusts

Generally speaking, charitable remainder trusts are classified into two types, depending upon how the income distributions are computed. In each case, the trust document will provide a stated percentage that is to be applied against the fair market value (FMV) of the account. This percentage must be between 5% and 50%. What distinguishes the two types is when the calculation is made.

Click each type to learn more.

 

Charitable Remainder Annuity Trust (CRAT)

The calculation is made one time only, against the initial FMV of the account. This results in a fixed distribution each year

Charitable Remainder Unitrust (CRUT)

The calculation is made annually, against the current FMV of the account.

 

Both are typically referred to by pronouncing their initials as if they formed words. The fact that the calculations are computed differently results in some different features that might make one or the other more appropriate for a given situation. These features are compared in the chart below.
