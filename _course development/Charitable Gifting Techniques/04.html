Income Tax Deductibility of Different Types of Property

There are numerous types of property that can be given to charities, but the most common are listed below. Generally speaking, the amount that can be deducted is either the fair market value (FMV) of the property or its basis (price paid before appreciation). In some cases, it is exclusively one or the other; in other cases, it is the lesser of the two.

The beauty of gifting appreciated property is that the donor pays no tax on the appreciation, yet can receive a charitable deduction for the appreciated amount. While property that has declined in value can be gifted (FMV is less than its basis), this is generally not a good idea because such gifts can only be deducted at the lesser FMV.
