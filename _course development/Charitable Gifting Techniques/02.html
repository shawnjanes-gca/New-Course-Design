Tax Benefit of Lifetime Charitable Gifts

One method that results in not paying any estate or gift taxes is to give everything to a qualified charity. Similar to the marital deduction, such transfers escape gift or estate taxation, and there is no limit on the amount or on the percentage of one's estate that may be deducted.

If the gift is made during one's lifetime, there is a potential savings on income taxes as well.

Example

Suppose a client with a taxable estate is thinking of making a charitable gift of $100,000 cash. The client's marginal rate for estate tax purposes is 40% and her income tax marginal rate is 39.6%. Assume the gift is fully deductible for income tax purposes. The client has three potential choices: keep the money, which allows the heirs to inherit it when the client dies; make the gift from the client's estate after death; or make a lifetime gift to the charity. As the example above shows, making the gift while alive and using the charitable income tax deduction saved an additional $39,600 in income taxes. But unlike the estate and gift tax deduction, which is unlimited, the income tax deduction may be limited.
