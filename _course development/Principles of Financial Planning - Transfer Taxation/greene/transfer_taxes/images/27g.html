What happens if there are no surviving lineal descendents?

In that case, this same adjustment to skip persons can be made for any of the descendents of the transferor's parents or the transferor's spouse's parents. But this only applies when the transferor has no lineal descendents of his/her own.

Example:

John has no lineal descendents. His brother, who died last year, was survived by one child and two grandchildren. If John were to make transfers to his deceased brother's grandchildren, they would no longer be considered skip persons.
