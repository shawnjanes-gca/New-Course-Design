Tax Impact of a Disqualifying Distribution

When there is a Disqualifying Distribution, the tax treatment to the employee will change depending on the strike price, the stock price at exercise, and the stock price at sale.

 

Sale Scenario

Tax Implications

Stock price at which the disqualified shares are sold is below the strike price (not a likely scenario, but possible)

Short-term capital loss

Stock price at which the disqualified shares are sold is greater than the strike price but less than or equal to the value at exercise

Compensation income

Stock price at which the disqualified shares are sold is greater than the strike price AND greater than the value at exercise.

The difference between the strike and the value at exercise is taxed as compensation income and the difference between the market value at exercise and the sale price is a capital gain.
