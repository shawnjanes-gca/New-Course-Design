Introduction

For years, the regulations governing distributions from an IRA and many other popular participant-directed plans were amazingly arcane and complex. Regulations were finalized in 2002 and 2006 cleared up many areas of confusion.

These rules dramatically altered the distribution requirements for IRA owners and employees participating in most qualified plans. At the core, the regulations provide client and advisors alike:

  • Simplicity in the calculation of the Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)

  • Flexibility for the IRA owner and the designated beneficiaries of the IRA

Upon reaching a specified age, minimum distributions are required each year.
