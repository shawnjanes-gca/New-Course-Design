Introduction

This course is part of a three-part series on federal taxation for Estate Planning. The first course examined the history of federal transfer taxes and the fundamental elements that are shared by both gift and estate taxes. This course builds upon the first by examining first what is unique about the taxation of gifts and then examining what is unique about the taxation of estates. The third course, which concludes this series, will examine Generation- Skipping Transfer taxes and specific elements of income taxes that are relevant to estate planning. The material in this course, along with the other two in the series, should be considered as prerequisite to any study of estate planning techniques.         

Objective

At the conclusion of this course, you will be able to:

  • Identify what constitutes a gift.
  • Explain the annual gift tax exclusion and other gift tax exclusions for special purpose gifts.
  • Distinguish between the probate estate and the taxable estate.
  • Identify assets that encompass the probate estate.
  • Understand how the gift and estate tax are calculated and how to estimate the potential transfer tax exposure.
  • Recognize the tax-exclusive nature of gift taxes vs. the tax-inclusive nature of estate taxes.
